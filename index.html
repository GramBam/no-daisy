No Daisy!

Dog training site for a specific naughty pup

Important Definitions
    ×

    Name & Explain

    Name & Explain is great method to help comfort the dog in a potentially stressful situation.

    You start by getting their attention, then calmly explain whats about to happen next, and how you want them to behave.

    An example of this would be if you hear noises outside the apartment and expect she might bark, say:

    "Daisy, thats just a person walking outside, I want you to be quiet and easy (relaxed)"

    ×

    Sit on the Dog

    Place Daisy on her leash, then sit on it, allowing her just enough length to lie quietly at your feet with a little bit of tension on the leash.

    Be sure to “sit on the dog” when you are working on something else: watching television, reading the newspaper, working on the computer. This exercise should be done for a minimum 30 minutes, at least once, and preferably twice a day.

    If she displays any un-relaxed behavior such as barking or whining (a very small amount of whining can be acceptable, she can't help it!) then you must reset the time back to 30 minutes.

    Continue until she is quiet and settled for 30 minutes straight.

    ×

    Bridging

    When Daisy is about to enter a stessful situation, you'll want to first name & explain, then say:

    "ready? good good good good good..."

    Continue saying "good" until the situation passes, then follow up with a "Good job!"

    An example of when you would use bridging is when you're about to pass by a stranger who has features that might trigger her

    ×

    Shake/Reset

    When Daisy is overly excited or stressed, she will often shake her body to calm herself down.

    ×

    Timeout

    When Daisy does a big nono (loud barking or excessive whinig), state: "timeout!" then put leash on and put her in the bathroom and close the door for a minimum of 20 seconds.

    You'll want to be on the otherside of the bathroom door holding the leash the entire time.

    Only let her out if she has completely stopped barking or whining.
